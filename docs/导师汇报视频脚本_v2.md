## PART 1：Problem（2分钟）

*画面：对着镜头讲*

"In a rural primary school in China, I teach English to 27 Grade 3 students, aged 8 to 9. As both the researcher and the English teacher, I face a core problem every day:

These children have almost zero exposure to English outside my classroom. When I say a word like 'bear,' only two or three students dare to respond. The rest stay silent — not because they don't want to learn, but because they are afraid to make mistakes, and I cannot support 27 students individually at the same time.

This is not just a motivation problem. It is a structural problem: the traditional classroom does not provide enough opportunities for each student to receive input, practice interaction, and produce output — all within a 40-minute lesson."

---

## PART 2：Solution Overview（3分钟）

*画面：切到PPT，展示图B（六理论层级架构图）*

"To address this problem, I developed a cooperative learning app grounded in six theoretical frameworks. These six theories were integrated in order to create a system where two students share one device, take different roles, and must work together to complete each task.

Let me show you how these theories are organized — not as a parallel list, but as a layered architecture where each layer answers a different design question."

### 【展示图B：Layered Theoretical Architecture】

*指着图，从底往上讲：*

**Layer 1 — WHY cooperate: Social Constructivism (Vygotsky, 1978)**

"The foundation layer determines the basic form of the app. Based on Vygotsky's social constructivism, knowledge is first constructed between people, then internalized by the individual. This theory is proposed to establish the cooperative structure: Student A and Student B must depend on each other. Neither can complete any task alone."

**Layer 2 — WHAT to do: SLA Theories (Krashen, Long, Swain)**

"The second layer determines the content of cooperation. Three SLA theories were integrated in order to guide what happens during the interaction:
- Krashen's Input Hypothesis (i+1) is proposed to control task difficulty — each task is slightly above the learner's current level, adjusted by an adaptive algorithm.
- Long's Interaction Hypothesis is proposed to create information gaps — Student A sees pictures but no words; Student B sees words but no pictures. This gap forces authentic negotiation of meaning.
- Swain's Output Hypothesis is proposed to push production — Student B must identify the English word; using Chinese will not work."

**Layer 3 — HOW to distribute: Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller, 1988)**

"The third layer determines how the task is split. Based on Sweller's Cognitive Load Theory, working memory is limited. This theory is proposed to reduce extraneous cognitive load by distributing the task: Student A processes audio and images; Student B processes images and meaning. Each student handles only half the information."

**Layer 4 — WHEN they fail: ZPD + Scaffolding (Vygotsky; Wood, Bruner & Ross, 1976)**

"The fourth layer determines what happens when students fail. Based on Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development, the app provides four levels of scaffolding that follow the contingent shift principle:
- Level 1: prompts peer discussion — using the partner as a More Knowledgeable Other
- Level 2: provides modified input — slower audio, highlighted keywords
- Level 3: offers linguistic hints — first letter, vocabulary translation
- Level 4: gives multimodal support — image, audio, text, and Chinese together

The scaffolding fades as the student succeeds. It is contingent, proportional, and temporary."

**Layer 5 — STRUCTURE: 3P Learning (Kilpatrick; Barrows; Hirsh-Pasek & Golinkoff)**

"The fifth layer determines how the entire learning system is organized at three levels:
- At the task level — Play-based Learning: each question is a game-like interaction (flipping cards, sorting words, relay challenges), not a traditional test item
- At the station level — Problem-based Learning: each station presents a problem that students must solve together before advancing
- At the unit level — Project-based Learning: each unit culminates in a collaborative project where students produce a meaningful artifact"

**Layer 6 — SUSTAIN motivation: Self-efficacy (Bandura, 1977)**

"The sixth layer determines how to keep students going. Based on Bandura's self-efficacy theory, the most powerful source of self-belief is mastery experience. The app visualizes success through stars and combo animations — these are not rewards, but visible evidence that 'I can do this.' This builds the confidence to continue."

### 【统一原则】

"Together, these six theories form a layered architecture. Each layer answers a design question that the others cannot answer. Remove any single layer, and a specific part of the system collapses:
- Without Social Constructivism → students work alone, no cooperation
- Without SLA → cooperation has no linguistic purpose
- Without CLT → both students process the same overloaded information
- Without Scaffolding → students get stuck with no support
- Without 3P → tasks are isolated exercises, not meaningful learning
- Without Self-efficacy → students give up after failure

This is what I mean by blend — not labeling, but structural dependency."

## PART 3：App Overview（3分钟）

*画面：切到App屏幕录制*

"Now let me show you how this architecture becomes a real app."

### 【进入App → 冒险地图】

"When students open the app, they enter an adventure map. This is the 3P structure made visible:
- Each circle on the map is a station — a Problem to solve
- Within each station, tasks are Play-based interactions
- The entire unit journey is a Project

The map shows four modules: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. Each module has four stations, progressing from easy to hard — this is Krashen's i+1 at the module level."

### 【选一个站点 → 进入】

"Let me enter the first Listening station: Listen Relay. Before we begin, notice: the app assigns roles — one student is A (blue), the other is B (orange). These roles automatically swap every question, ensuring both students practice all skills equally."

## PART 4：One Question, Six Theories（5分钟）

*画面：切到PPT，展示图C（一道题六理论环形图），然后切回App演示*

"Now I will walk you through one single question — 30 seconds of interaction — and show you how all six theories work simultaneously."

### 【展示图C：One Question, All Theories】

*简要指着图说明六个理论在这道题里的位置，然后切到App实际操作*

### 【Step 1：A's Screen】

*操作：播放音频，A看到四张图片*

"Student A hears the audio: 'bear.' A sees four pictures — no English words, only images.

Where are the theories?
- **CLT**: A processes only audio + images. No text. Extraneous load removed.
- **SLA i+1**: the vocabulary is at A's current level, selected by the adaptive algorithm.
- **Play**: A is choosing a picture, not answering a test — it feels like a game."

### 【Step 2：B's Screen Activates】

*操作：A选完，B的区域亮起*

"The moment A selects a picture, B's section activates. B sees the picture A chose, and four English word options. B must select the correct word.

Where are the theories?
- **Social Constructivism**: B cannot start without A's input. The structure enforces cooperation.
- **SLA Interaction (Long)**: A had pictures but no words. B has words but doesn't know which picture A heard. This information gap creates real communicative need — they must talk to each other.
- **SLA Output (Swain)**: B must recognize and select the English word. Chinese is not an option. B is pushed to engage with English output.
- **CLT**: B processes image + word meaning. Different from A's audio + image. Each handles half."

### 【Step 3：答错 → 支架触发】

*操作：故意选错，展示四级支架*

"If the pair answers incorrectly, watch what happens.

First attempt wrong → Level 1 scaffold appears: a yellow banner says 'Discuss with your partner first.' This is ZPD — using the peer as support before the system intervenes.

Still wrong → Level 2: the audio replays at 0.8x speed with keywords highlighted. This is modified input — not repeating the same thing, but presenting it differently.

Still wrong → Level 3: the first letter of the word appears, plus a vocabulary hint.

Still wrong → Level 4: full multimodal support — picture highlighted, audio replayed, Chinese translation shown.

This is scaffolding as Wood, Bruner and Ross defined it: increase support when the learner fails, decrease when they succeed. Contingent. Proportional. Temporary."

### 【Step 4：答对 → Combo + 星星】

*操作：答对，看动画*

"When they answer correctly — especially consecutively — the app shows a combo animation and awards stars.

This is Bandura's self-efficacy: mastery experience, celebrated in the moment. The stars are not a reward system. They are visible evidence of competence — 'We got it right. We can do this.' This builds the belief that drives continued effort."

### 【Step 5：下一题自适应】

*操作：进入下一题*

"The next question is automatically adjusted. If they answered correctly, the adaptive algorithm increases difficulty slightly — Krashen's i+1 in real time. If they struggled, difficulty decreases. The system finds each pair's zone of proximal development, dynamically."

## PART 5：Teacher's Role（2分钟）

*画面：切到教师Dashboard或回到镜头*

"While students work in pairs on the app, I am not passive. As the teacher, I serve as an additional layer of scaffolding.

The teacher dashboard shows me in real time:
- Which pair has finished
- Which pair is stuck
- Which pair used Level 3 or 4 scaffolds on which question

This allows me to walk over, observe, and provide human scaffolding — exactly where and when it is needed.

The app does not replace the teacher. It creates 13 simultaneous cooperative learning spaces, each with built-in scaffolding. I monitor and intervene at the moments that matter."

## PART 6：Closing — Blend, Not Labels（1分钟）

*画面：回到图B层级图*

"Let me return to the theoretical framework.

What you just saw was one question — 30 seconds of student interaction. In those 30 seconds:
- Social Constructivism determined the cooperative structure
- Three SLA theories guided the content and interaction
- CLT distributed the cognitive load
- Scaffolding responded to failure dynamically
- Play-based design made the task engaging
- Self-efficacy turned success into confidence

These theories were not applied after the app was designed. They were integrated in order to determine every design decision from the beginning. Each theory answers a question that no other theory can answer. Remove any one, and the system is incomplete.

This is my answer to the question: how do I blend all selected theories to develop this app?"
