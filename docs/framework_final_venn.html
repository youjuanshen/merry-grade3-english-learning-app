Note.
SC = Social Constructivism (Vygotsky, 1978) — knowledge is constructed through social interaction, not transmitted from teacher to learner. In this study, SC provides the epistemological rationale for cooperative learning between two students sharing one device.
ZPD = Zone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky, 1978) — the developmental distance between what a learner can do independently and what they can achieve with guidance. All learning activities in this app are calibrated to fall within this range.
Scaffolding (Wood, Bruner, & Ross, 1976) — contingent, temporary support that is gradually withdrawn as learner competence grows. Operationalized in three tiers: Peer negotiation → AI-mediated hints → Teacher intervention.
SLA = Second Language Acquisition — three complementary principles guide language learning design: comprehensible input one step beyond current level (Krashen, 1985), negotiation of meaning through interaction (Long, 1996), and pushed output that prompts learners to notice gaps in their own language ability (Swain, 1985). Adopted as design heuristics; this study does not commit to the full theoretical position of any single hypothesis.
3P = Play-based, Problem-based, and Project-based Learning (synthesized in this study from Hirsh-Pasek et al.; Barrows, 1986; Kilpatrick, 1918) — a progressive activity design moving from guided recognition (Play) through contextual application (Problem) to creative production (Project); cf. Gradual Release of Responsibility (Pearson & Gallagher, 1983).
CLT = Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller, 1988) — learning is optimized when extraneous cognitive load is minimized. In this study, each task is split between Students A and B, reducing individual load while the sequential dependency (A acts first, B responds) necessitates interaction.
SE = Self-efficacy (Bandura, 1977) — an individual's belief in their capability to succeed at a given task, shaped by four sources: mastery experience, vicarious experience, verbal persuasion, and physiological states. This study centrally applies the first two: mastery experience through progressive task completion with mandatory error correction, and vicarious experience through shared-screen observation where Student B watches Student A perform.
AI assistance operates at three levels: (1) AI-powered development — content, question banks, and adaptive logic designed with LLM assistance; (2) AI-driven delivery — text-to-speech synthesis (TTS) and spoken English oral evaluation (SOE) powered by cloud-based AI services; (3) AI-informed adaptation — rule-based algorithm implementing i+1 difficulty adjustment and scaffold triggering logic, designed with AI assistance. Activity flow and A/B task distribution are pre-programmed design decisions, not AI-driven processes.
This framework is grounded in SC as its epistemological foundation, with ZPD defining the developmental space in which all design mechanisms operate. Within ZPD, three intertwined mechanisms work simultaneously in every learning task: SLA guides the language learning principles, 3P organizes how activities progress from guided to independent, and CLT regulates how cognitive demands are distributed between two learners. SE operates as a cross-cutting motivational design layer, ensuring that task completion fosters learners' belief in their own capabilities.
